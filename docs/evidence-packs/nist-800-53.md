## 1. Purpose of this evidence pack

NIST Special Publication 800-53 Revision 5 (Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations) is the comprehensive catalogue of federal information-security and privacy controls. Mandated for US federal agencies under the Federal Information Security Modernization Act (FISMA) via FIPS 200 and SP 800-53B control baselines (Low, Moderate, High). Extensively referenced by private-sector frameworks (SOC 2, CIS Controls) and cloud regulations (FedRAMP, CMMC).

## 2. Scope and applicability

US federal agencies and their contractors processing federal information under FISMA. Baselines: SP 800-53B Low (for FIPS 199 Low systems), Moderate, High. Privacy controls (appended to 800-53 in Rev. 5) apply to systems processing PII. CUI-specific controls in SP 800-171 are derived from 800-53.

**Territorial scope.** US federal scope; global contractor applicability where US government data is involved. Frequently adopted voluntarily by non-federal organisations.

## 5. Evidence collection

### 5.1 Common evidence sources

Auditors typically request the following records when examining this regulation:

- System inventory and FIPS 199 categorisation records
- Configuration-management tooling (Ansible, Chef, Puppet) with version control
- Vulnerability-scan outputs tied to SP 800-53 RA-5
- SIEM events tagged with 800-53 control references
- Access-review and account-management records (AC-2)
- Encryption configuration evidence (SC-8, SC-13, SC-28)
- Incident-response records (IR-6)
- Continuous-monitoring dashboards (CA-7)

### 5.2 Retention requirements

| Artifact | Retention | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| System Security Plan (SSP) | Duration of system lifecycle + 3 years post-decommission | NIST SP 800-37 Rev. 2; NARA GRS 4.2 |
| Authorisation To Operate (ATO) package | 3 years post-ATO expiration (6 years for CUI systems) | OMB A-130; NARA GRS 4.2 |
| Audit records (AU-11) | Organisation-defined; FedRAMP minimum 1 year online + 3 years archived | SP 800-53 Rev. 5 AU-11; FedRAMP guidance |
| Incident-response records (IR-6) | 3 years; 6 years for classified or high-impact systems | SP 800-61 Rev. 2; NARA GRS 4.2 |
| Continuous-monitoring records (CA-7) | 3 years rolling | SP 800-137 |
| Privacy-impact assessments (AR-1) | Duration of system + 3 years | SP 800-53 Rev. 5; OMB A-130 |

> Retention figures above are the legal minimums or regulator-stated expectations. Organisation-specific retention schedules may be longer where business, tax, litigation-hold, or contractual obligations apply. Where a figure conflicts with local data-protection law (e.g. GDPR Art.5(1)(e) storage-limitation principle), the shorter conformant period governs for personal-data content; the evidence-of-compliance retention retains the longer period for audit purposes, scrubbed of excess personal data.

### 5.3 Evidence integrity expectations

Regulators increasingly cite **evidence-integrity failures** as aggravating factors in enforcement actions. Cross-regulation baseline expectations:

- Time-stamped, tamper-evident storage (WORM, cryptographic chaining, or append-only indexes).
- Chain-of-custody for any evidence removed from the SIEM / production system for audit or legal purposes.
- Synchronised clocks (NTP stratum ‚â§ 3 or equivalent) across all in-scope sources so timeline reconstruction is defensible.
- Documented retention enforcement ‚Äî not just retention policy ‚Äî so that deletion is auditable.

See cat-22.35 "Evidence continuity and log integrity" for UCs that implement these controls.

## 6. Control testing procedures

Assessment methodology in SP 800-53A uses four methods: Examine, Interview, Test, Analyse. Each control has assessment objectives, procedures, and expected evidence. Security Control Assessors (SCAs) sample controls based on FIPS 199 impact level and produce a Security Assessment Report (SAR) that feeds into the ATO decision. Continuous monitoring under SP 800-137 replaces point-in-time assessments for ongoing authorisation.

**Reporting cadence.** ATO typically 3-year cycle (agency-specific). Continuous monitoring is ongoing; metrics reporting to OMB and agency CISO quarterly. FISMA annual reporting by agency CISO to OMB via CyberScope.

## 7. Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Authorizing Official (AO)** | Federal official with authority to issue ATO; accepts risk on behalf of the agency. |
| **Information System Owner** | Responsible for procurement, deployment, operation, and security of the system. |
| **Information System Security Officer (ISSO)** | Day-to-day security operations and documentation (SSP, POA&M). |

## 9. Common audit deficiencies

Findings frequently cited by regulators, certification bodies, and external auditors for this regulation. These should be pre-tested as part of readiness reviews.

- Control selection deviates from SP 800-53B baseline without documented tailoring rationale.
- POA&M items age beyond allowed remediation windows without formal extensions.
- Continuous-monitoring metrics are collected but not analysed or escalated.
- Privacy controls (AR, DM, IP, SE, TR, UL families) are not implemented for systems processing PII.
- Inherited controls from cloud providers (e.g. FedRAMP Authorized CSPs) are assumed rather than tested.
- Event-log configuration (AU-2, AU-3) excludes privileged events or administrative actions.

## 10. Enforcement and penalties

FISMA has no direct monetary penalty, but: non-compliance blocks ATO (system cannot operate); OMB can withhold budget; GAO/OIG audits flag agencies in annual reports. For contractors under CUI obligations (SP 800-171), DFARS 252.204-7012 includes False Claims Act exposure for misrepresented compliance.

## 12. Questions an auditor should ask

These are the questions a regulator, certification body, or external auditor is likely to ask. The pack helps preparers stage evidence and pre-test responses before the review opens.

- Produce the current System Security Plan (SSP) and the list of applicable controls from SP 800-53B per the system's categorisation.
- Demonstrate the assessment of each control under the ISO 17020-style independent assessor regime (SP 800-53A).
- Produce the Security Assessment Report (SAR) and the Plan of Actions & Milestones (POA&M) with remediation evidence.
- Show the continuous-monitoring program output for the last 12 months ‚Äî metrics, deviations, corrective actions.
- For AU-2 (Audit Events), produce the event-type list and demonstrate logs are generated for each.
- For CM-3 (Configuration Change Control), produce the last five configuration changes and the CCB approvals.
- For SC-8 (Transmission Confidentiality and Integrity), demonstrate cryptographic protection on every in-scope transmission.
- For PM-14 (Testing, Training, and Monitoring), produce training-completion records and testing evidence.
