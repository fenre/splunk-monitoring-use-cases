## 1. Purpose of this evidence pack

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). It specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving a risk-based ISMS. The 2022 revision (formally ISO/IEC 27001:2022(E)) replaces the 2013 edition, aligns with the 2022 update to ISO/IEC 27002 control set (93 controls in 4 themes: Organisational, People, Physical, Technological). Certification is issued by accredited certification bodies; organisations have until 31 October 2025 to transition certifications from 2013 to 2022.

## 2. Scope and applicability

Any organisation (commercial, public-sector, not-for-profit) wishing to demonstrate information-security maturity to customers, regulators, or internal stakeholders. Certification scope is defined by the organisation in the Statement of Applicability (SoA) and Scope Statement; full-enterprise certifications and narrow product/service certifications are both permitted.

**Territorial scope.** Global. Accredited certification bodies operate under ISO/IEC 17021-1 and IAF MLAs, providing cross-border certification recognition.

## 5. Evidence collection

### 5.1 Common evidence sources

Auditors typically request the following records when examining this regulation:

- Risk register (master document with risk IDs, owners, treatments)
- SoA (Statement of Applicability) with inclusion/exclusion rationale
- Internal-audit programme and reports
- Management-review minutes with action tracking
- Control-operation records (change logs, access-review minutes, incident tickets)
- Awareness-training completion records
- Supplier / third-party risk-assessment records (A.5.19-23)
- Vulnerability-management records (A.8.8)

### 5.2 Retention requirements

| Artifact | Retention | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| Statement of Applicability (SoA) | Indefinite (core ISMS record) | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.1.3 d) |
| Risk-assessment and risk-treatment records | Minimum certification cycle (3 years) + 1 surveillance period | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.1.2 |
| Internal-audit reports | Minimum certification cycle (3 years) + 1 surveillance period | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.2 |
| Management-review minutes | Minimum certification cycle (3 years) + 1 surveillance period | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 9.3 |
| Control-operation evidence (per SoA applicable controls) | 12 months minimum (surveillance cycle) | ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 7.5.3 implicit via Annex A.5.33 |
| Audit-log records (Annex A.8.15) | Organisation-defined based on risk; typically 12-36 months | ISO/IEC 27002:2022 A.8.15 guidance |

> Retention figures above are the legal minimums or regulator-stated expectations. Organisation-specific retention schedules may be longer where business, tax, litigation-hold, or contractual obligations apply. Where a figure conflicts with local data-protection law (e.g. GDPR Art.5(1)(e) storage-limitation principle), the shorter conformant period governs for personal-data content; the evidence-of-compliance retention retains the longer period for audit purposes, scrubbed of excess personal data.

### 5.3 Evidence integrity expectations

Regulators increasingly cite **evidence-integrity failures** as aggravating factors in enforcement actions. Cross-regulation baseline expectations:

- Time-stamped, tamper-evident storage (WORM, cryptographic chaining, or append-only indexes).
- Chain-of-custody for any evidence removed from the SIEM / production system for audit or legal purposes.
- Synchronised clocks (NTP stratum ≤ 3 or equivalent) across all in-scope sources so timeline reconstruction is defensible.
- Documented retention enforcement — not just retention policy — so that deletion is auditable.

See cat-22.35 "Evidence continuity and log integrity" for UCs that implement these controls.

## 6. Control testing procedures

Certification audits follow ISO/IEC 17021-1: Stage 1 (documentation review) + Stage 2 (on-site effectiveness audit). Surveillance audits are annual, re-certification every three years. Auditors sample controls from the SoA, test evidence of operation throughout the audit period, and issue findings (major non-conformity, minor non-conformity, observation, opportunity for improvement). Major non-conformities suspend certification until remediated.

**Reporting cadence.** 3-year certification cycle: Stage 1 + Stage 2 at initial certification, annual surveillance audits in years 1 and 2, re-certification audit in year 3 (covers full scope again). Certificates expire 3 years from issue; transition certificates issued between 2022-2025 must be re-issued against the 2022 standard by 31 October 2025.

## 9. Common audit deficiencies

Findings frequently cited by regulators, certification bodies, and external auditors for this regulation. These should be pre-tested as part of readiness reviews.

- SoA lists Annex A controls as 'applicable' but evidence of operation is absent or stale.
- Risk assessment is performed once at certification and not updated for significant changes (M&A, new products, cloud migrations).
- Internal-audit programme covers the same domains repeatedly and misses less-visible areas (e.g. physical security of regional offices).
- Management review is performed but actions are not tracked to closure.
- Supplier-security controls (A.5.19-23) rely on contractual-clause evidence with no operational monitoring.
- Transition from 27001:2013 to 27001:2022 is paper-only; new controls (e.g. A.8.25 secure development, A.8.28 secure coding) are not genuinely implemented.

## 10. Enforcement and penalties

ISO 27001 has no statutory penalty; enforcement is commercial and reputational. Certificate suspension or withdrawal typically causes customer contract breaches, loss of tender eligibility, and regulatory escalation (some regulators, e.g. NIS2 Art.21(5), reference ISO 27001 as evidence of adequate controls). Certification bodies can withdraw accreditation from non-conforming customers.

## 12. Questions an auditor should ask

These are the questions a regulator, certification body, or external auditor is likely to ask. The pack helps preparers stage evidence and pre-test responses before the review opens.

- Produce the Scope Statement and Statement of Applicability (SoA); reconcile every Annex A control with its applicability decision and justification.
- Demonstrate the information-security risk-assessment process; show the last full risk-assessment and any material updates since.
- Produce the Risk Treatment Plan and evidence that accepted residual risks are signed off at the appropriate management level.
- Show evidence of operation for every Annex A control that is 'applicable' in the SoA, sampling from the last 12 months.
- Produce the internal-audit programme and the results of the last annual cycle; demonstrate non-conformity closure.
- Produce management-review minutes and evidence of ISMS-improvement actions tracked to closure.
- Demonstrate information-security awareness training is delivered to all personnel with ISMS responsibilities (Clause 7.3); sample completion records.
- Show that the ISMS is integrated with the organisation's business processes (Clause 5.1) rather than operating as a standalone compliance activity.
