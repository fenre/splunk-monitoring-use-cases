## 1. Purpose of this evidence pack

IEC 61511 (2016, Edition 2) "Functional safety — Safety Instrumented Systems for the process industry sector" is the international standard governing Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS) in the process industries (oil & gas, refining, petrochemicals, chemicals, pharmaceuticals, power, water-and-wastewater, mining, pulp-and-paper, and certain food-processing operations). IEC 61511 operationalises IEC 61508 (the more abstract parent standard) for the process-sector domain, defining the 16-phase SIS safety lifecycle, the Safety Integrity Level (SIL) construct (SIL-1 to SIL-4 — most process-sector SIS claim SIL-2 or SIL-3), and the documentation, verification, and Functional Safety Assessment (FSA) duties at each lifecycle phase.

IEC 61511 Edition 2 (2016) introduced a critical cybersecurity clause — **Clause 8.2.4** — which mandates a SIS Cybersecurity Risk Assessment (CRA) using a recognised methodology. The recognised methodology is **ISA-TR84.00.09 (2017)** "Cybersecurity Related to the Functional Safety Lifecycle", which extends the SIS lifecycle with cybersecurity hazard analysis and integrates with the broader IEC 62443 industrial-control-system cybersecurity family (specifically IEC 62443-3-2:2020 for zone-and-conduit analysis and IEC 62443-3-3:2013 for the seven Foundational Requirements: FR1 Identification & Authentication Control, FR2 Use Control, FR3 System Integrity, FR4 Data Confidentiality, FR5 Restricted Data Flow, FR6 Timely Response to Events, FR7 Resource Availability).

The IEC 61511 + ISA-TR84.00.09 + IEC 62443 stack is not a legal regulation by itself. It is, however, the universally-recognised "Good Engineering Practice" (GEP) for Safety Instrumented Systems and is incorporated by reference into many legal regimes:

- **United States** — OSHA Process Safety Management (29 CFR 1910.119), via the "recognised and generally accepted good engineering practice" (RAGAGEP) doctrine; EPA Risk Management Program (40 CFR 68); CFATS for chemical-sector security.
- **United Kingdom** — HSE COMAH 2015 (the UK implementation of Seveso III); HSE SPC/Tech/OSD/30 guidance.
- **European Union** — Seveso III (Directive 2012/18/EU) "Major-Accident Hazards"; ATEX 2014/34/EU for explosive-atmosphere instruments; the NIS2 / CER overlap for OT-cyber on critical infrastructure.
- **India** — MSIHC Rules 1989 (Manufacture, Storage and Import of Hazardous Chemicals) under the Environment (Protection) Act 1986; PESO orders.
- **Korea** — Process Safety Management under the Occupational Safety and Health Act + KOSHA P-91-2017 guidance.
- **Australia** — Hazardous-Industry Major-Hazard-Facilities (MHF) regulations under state Work-Health-and-Safety law; Safe Work Australia.
- **Brazil** — NR-20 (flammable and combustible liquids), NR-23 (fire prevention) under the Ministry of Labour.
- **Insurance / finance** — D&O, environmental-liability, and process-safety insurers routinely require IEC 61511 conformance as a coverage precondition. Lloyd's-syndicate underwriters specifically look for the FSA documentation chain.
- **Vendor / Original-Design-Manufacturer (ODM)** — Major SIS vendors (HIMA, Siemens TÜV-certified, Honeywell, Emerson, Yokogawa, Schneider, ABB) increasingly require IEC 62443-4-2 certification on their own products.

The Splunk monitoring catalogue's coverage maps directly into this stack: the catalogue produces an auditable evidence chain that the Functional Safety Manager can hand to a process-safety auditor (PSM / COMAH / Seveso / MSIHC), an insurance underwriter's risk engineer, or the certifying body (TÜV, exida, DEKRA, Bureau Veritas, Lloyd's Register).

## 2. Scope and applicability

Applies to:

- **Every Safety Instrumented System (SIS) in the process industries** — oil & gas (upstream / midstream / downstream), refining, petrochemicals, specialty chemicals, pharmaceuticals (where SIS-protected reactors / dryers / mills are deployed), power generation (boiler-protection, turbine-protection, generator-step-up-protection), water-and-wastewater (high-hazard chemical-dosing and digester-protection), mining (process-protection on flotation and tailings circuits), pulp-and-paper (digester protection), food-processing (SIS-protected industrial dryers, sterilisers).
- **Every Safety Instrumented Function (SIF)** implemented within those SIS — covering the sensor(s) → logic-solver → final-element loop.
- **The operating organisation** at every IEC 61511 lifecycle phase — design (TC-holder / engineering contractor), operations, maintenance, and decommissioning.
- **The Functional Safety Manager (FSM)** designated under IEC 61511 Clause 5; the SIS-Senior-Competent-Engineer.
- **Every Cybersecurity-related design or operational change** within the SIS zone or its conduits under IEC 61511 Clause 8.2.4 + Clause 17 (Management of Change).

Excludes: BPCS (Basic Process Control System) protection that is not claimed as a SIF; pure machinery-safety covered by IEC 62061 / ISO 13849 (machinery functional-safety); rail signalling (IEC 62278/62279); medical-device functional-safety (IEC 60601 family); nuclear (IEC 61513 + IEC 60880); automotive (ISO 26262); aviation (DO-178C + ARP4754A).

**Territorial scope.** Global, via the legal-regime incorporation paths listed in §1. Recognition is universal — the IEC 61511 standard is identical worldwide; national-regulator differences come in the legal-regime overlay and the FSA-cycle / FSA-independence requirements (e.g. OSHA-recognised vs HSE-recognised assessors).

## 5. Evidence collection

### 5.1 Common evidence sources

- **SIS Lifecycle Register** — KV Store `iec_61511_sis_lifecycle_register_lookup`. The 16-phase SIS safety lifecycle per SIS, with deliverable-completion / verification / FSA tracking.
- **SIS CRA Register** — KV Store `iec_61511_sis_cra_register_lookup`. Per-SIS Cybersecurity Risk Assessment with methodology, assessor credential, evidence references.
- **SIS CRA Findings Register** — KV Store `iec_61511_cra_findings_lookup`. Open CRA-driven findings with severity, owner, age, remediation status.
- **SIS Override Procedure Register** — KV Store `iec_61511_sis_override_procedure_lookup`. Authorised override / bypass procedures, time bounds, authoriser.
- **SIS-zone / SIS-conduit register** — KV Store `iec_62443_zone_lookup`. Per-zone SL-T allocations across FR1-FR7.
- **Component SL-C register** — KV Store `iec_62443_component_sl_capability_lookup`. Per-component SL-C across FR1-FR7 with vendor certification reference (ISA Secure / TÜV / exida).
- **SL-A measurement register** — KV Store `iec_62443_sl_achieved_measurement_lookup`. Periodic (typically biennial) SL-A measurements by accredited 62443 assessor.
- **SL exception register** — KV Store `iec_62443_sl_exception_register_lookup`. Documented SL-gap exceptions with compensating control + business-acceptance + expiry.
- **SIS proof-test register** — KV Store `iec_61511_sif_proof_test_register_lookup`. Per-SIF design proof-test interval + PFD budget + design demand-rate.
- **SIS proof-test event register** — KV Store `iec_61511_proof_test_event_lookup`. CMMS-derived proof-test executions (Maximo / SAP-PM / Infor EAM).
- **SIS MoC register** — KV Store `iec_61511_sis_moc_register_lookup`. Every SIS-affecting Management-of-Change with classification, SIL-impact, CRA-refresh, PSSR closure.
- **SIS-relevant cyber-event register** — KV Store `iec_61511_sis_relevant_cyber_event_register_lookup`. Every SIS-zone / SIS-conduit cyber-event with SLA timestamps and CRA-link.
- **SIS logic-solver demand event** — `index=ot_sis sourcetype=sis:demand`. Captured via vendor TA (HIMA, Siemens TÜV-certified, Honeywell Safety Manager, Emerson DeltaV SIS, Yokogawa ProSafe-RS, Schneider Triconex, ABB AC 800M HI).
- **SIS logic-solver spurious-trip event** — `index=ot_sis sourcetype=sis:spurious_trip`. Same vendor-TA sources.
- **OT-Security detection events with SIS-zone tagging** — `index=ot_security sis_relevance=true` from Claroty / Dragos / Nozomi / Tenable.ot.
- **CMMS work-order export** — IBM Maximo / SAP-PM / Infor EAM for proof-test executions and findings.
- **Functional-safety GRC platform** — exida exSILentia / Hima ExSILent / Smart Project for the SIS-SRS, LOPA, SIL Verification, FSA reports.

### 5.3 Evidence integrity expectations

Functional-safety evidence is expected to follow strict change-control with explicit version-history. The Splunk catalogue archives every UC-22.63.x result row to the `audit_evidence` summary index with a stable marker (`uc=22.63.X,reg=IEC-61511,clause=...`). Recommended pattern: RFC 3161 time-stamping via DigiCert / DocuSign; functional-safety GRC platform (exida exSILentia / Hima ExSILent / Smart Project) immutable-change-history mode; ServiceNow GRC for MoC and PSSR records. Critical sign-offs (SIS-SRS approval; LOPA SIL allocation; FSA closure; CRA closure; SIS-affecting MoC closure) should be physically signed by the FSM and counter-signed by the Process Safety Manager and the Plant Manager.

## 6. Control testing procedures

### 6.1 Auditor-style testing

A process-safety auditor (PSM / COMAH / Seveso / MSIHC), an insurance risk-engineer, or a certifying-body auditor typically tests:

- **SIS lifecycle phase completion** — pick a named SIS; demonstrate the 16-phase lifecycle register, the deliverable-completion / verification / FSA chain (UC-22.63.1).
- **SIS CRA freshness** — pick a named SIS; demonstrate the current CRA report, methodology alignment with ISA-TR84.00.09 / IEC 62443-3-2, the open-finding remediation status (UC-22.63.2).
- **SIS-BPCS separation + override logging** — pick a named SIF; demonstrate the SIS-BPCS network separation evidence and the override/bypass log with proper authorisation (UC-22.63.3).
- **SIS proof-test status** — pick a named SIF; demonstrate the design proof-test interval, the last proof-test, the observed demand rate, and the spurious-trip count (UC-22.63.4).
- **SIS MoC discipline** — pick a recent SIS-affecting change; demonstrate the classification, the SIL-impact assessment, the CRA refresh (if cyber-relevant), the PSSR, and the lifecycle-deliverable updates (UC-22.63.5).
- **Integrated cybersecurity programme** — synthetic SIS-zone cyber-event; demonstrate the 5-minute acknowledgement + 8-hour CRA link + 24-hour PHA-decision SLA (UC-22.63.6).
- **IEC 62443-3-2 SL evidence** — pick a named SIS zone; demonstrate the SL-T allocation, the per-component SL-C certification, the latest SL-A measurement, and the exception register (UC-22.63.7).

### 6.2 Internal Functional-Safety-Manager testing

Quarterly self-test (Functional Safety Manager + Process Safety Manager + Plant Manager led):

1. Pick a random SIS-affecting MoC from the last 90 days. Audit every workflow step (classification → SIL-impact → CRA refresh → PSSR → lifecycle-deliverable update).
2. Trigger a synthetic SIS-zone cyber-event matching UC-22.63.6. Verify SLA conformance and record in `audit_evidence` with `iec_61511_exercise_id=Q...`.
3. Verify proof-test execution for every L3+ SIF in the past 6 months (UC-22.63.4).
4. Run UC-22.63.7 over every operating SIS zone to surface unresolved SL gaps.

## 7. Roles and responsibilities

- **Functional Safety Manager (FSM)** — Designated under IEC 61511 Clause 5; owns the SIS safety lifecycle. Should hold a recognised IEC 61511 / IEC 61508 competence certificate (TÜV FSEng / CFSE).
- **Process Safety Manager** — Owns the broader process-safety management system per OSHA PSM / COMAH / Seveso / MSIHC, and integrates the SIS lifecycle with PHA / LOPA / MoC / PSSR / training.
- **Plant Manager** — Counter-signs SIS-affecting MoC closure and the annual FSM attestation; bears overall responsibility for the operating safety case.
- **OT Security Lead** — Owns the SIS-zone-and-conduit security implementation, IEC 62443 SL-T allocation, and the integrated cybersecurity programme.
- **Reliability Engineer** — Owns the proof-test workflow, demand-rate tracking, and spurious-trip-rate trending.
- **Maintenance Manager / CMMS Owner** — Owns the CMMS instance of proof-test work-orders and the maintenance-organisation discipline.
- **PHA / LOPA Team Lead** — Owns the LOPA / SIL allocation revision cadence and the integration with cyber-event feedback (ISA-TR84.00.09 §4).
- **Independent Functional Safety Assessor (FSA)** — Required under IEC 61511 Clause 5.2.6 for SIL-2 (Stage 3 + Stage 4) and SIL-3+ (all 5 stages). Independence requirements: separate from the design and operations teams; the operating organisation cannot assess its own SIS lifecycle for SIL-3+.
- **Accredited IEC 62443 assessor** — Required for SL-A measurement (typically biennial).
- **Board Safety Committee** — Receives quarterly integrated-cybersecurity-programme SLA metrics from UC-22.63.6.

## 9. Common audit deficiencies

1. **SIS-affecting MoC misclassification as replacement-in-kind** — Most consistent root-cause finding across major process-safety incidents (Williams Olefins 2013, West Fertilizer 2013, others). Mitigated by UC-22.63.5.
2. **CRA stale (>5 years)** — Operating organisations conduct a CRA at commissioning and never refresh it. Mitigated by UC-22.63.2.
3. **Proof-test overdue for L3+ SIFs** — The most consistent reliability-related audit finding; the SIL claim becomes defensible-only-on-paper. Mitigated by UC-22.63.4.
4. **SIS overrides without time-bounded authorisation** — Maintenance overrides exceed the documented procedure window. Mitigated by UC-22.63.3.
5. **SIS-zone cyber-event not feeding back into CRA / PHA** — ISA-TR84.00.09 §4 limb absent or partial. Mitigated by UC-22.63.6.
6. **IEC 62443-3-2 SL-T over-allocation** — Operating organisations allocate SL-T = 3 across every zone without zone-specific risk justification, leading to costly capex without genuine risk-reduction. Mitigated by UC-22.63.7 via the rubric guidance.
7. **SL-A measurement stale** — A commissioning-day SL-A measurement persists without periodic re-measurement. Mitigated by UC-22.63.7.
8. **Inadequate FSA independence at SIL-3+** — The operating organisation conducts the FSA itself rather than via an independent assessor. Mitigated by UC-22.63.1.
9. **Lifecycle deliverable gaps post-MoC** — A MoC closes but the SIS-SRS / design documents are not updated. Mitigated by UC-22.63.5.
10. **PSSR open findings on restart** — A change is implemented before all PSSR findings are closed. Mitigated by UC-22.63.5.

## 10. Enforcement and penalties

IEC 61511 is a voluntary international standard; enforcement comes via the legal-regime overlay:

- **OSHA PSM (29 CFR 1910.119)** — administrative penalties up to USD 145,027 per "wilful" violation (2024 adjusted); criminal referral for fatal incidents (typical felony charge: 29 U.S.C. § 666(e)).
- **EPA RMP (40 CFR 68)** — administrative penalties up to USD 117,468 per day per violation; criminal referral for releases with reckless endangerment.
- **HSE COMAH 2015** — unlimited fines on conviction; imprisonment up to 2 years for senior management on indictment.
- **Seveso III** — penalties under Member-State implementing law; typical EU national maxima: EUR 1,000,000-5,000,000 per breach + senior-management criminal liability.
- **MSIHC Rules 1989** — penalties under the Environment (Protection) Act 1986 — fine up to INR 100,000 + imprisonment up to 5 years (Section 15).
- **Insurance** — denial of coverage on D&O, environmental-liability, and process-safety policies for non-conformant operations is the most consequential financial penalty in practice.
- **Vendor / ODM contracts** — major SIS vendors increasingly contractually require IEC 62443-4-2 certification on integrators; non-conformance voids warranty.

## 11. Questions a process-safety auditor should ask

- Show me the SIS lifecycle register for [named SIS]. Demonstrate every phase, the deliverable, the verification, and the FSA stage closure.
- For [named L3+ SIF] — show me the design proof-test interval, the most recent proof-test, the observed demand rate, and the spurious-trip count for the last 24 months.
- Pick a recent SIS-affecting MoC. Walk me through the classification, the SIL-impact assessment, the CRA refresh (if cyber-relevant), and the PSSR closure.
- For [named SIS zone] — show me the IEC 62443-3-2 SL-T allocation, every in-zone component's SL-C certification, the most recent SL-A measurement, and any active exceptions.
- For a recent SIS-zone cyber-event — show me the acknowledgement timestamp, the CRA-link timestamp, and the PHA-refresh-decision timestamp.
- Show me the SIS override / bypass log for the past 90 days. Identify any that exceeded the authorised time window or lacked a documented justification.
- For an L3+ SIF — confirm the FSA was conducted by an independent assessor (separate from the design and operations teams).
