# Auditor discovery interview guide

> **Status:** Phase 0.1 draft, produced 2026-04-16.
> **Purpose:** structured 45-minute interview guide for auditors, DPOs, QSAs
> and SOC-2 practitioners. Findings populate
> `docs/auditor-research/findings.md` and drive the regulatory-gold-standard
> schema and evidence-pack requirements in Phase 1.
> **Consent:** every interview is conducted under the consent / anonymisation
> language in section 1; responses are aggregated before publication.

## 1. Opening (5 min)

### 1.1 Consent & framing script

> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm researching what would make a Splunk
> monitoring catalogue genuinely useful for you during an audit. This is a
> 45-minute structured interview. I'll take notes, but your name, firm and
> client names will never appear anywhere — results are aggregated before we
> publish, and you get the draft summary before it goes out. Is it OK if I
> proceed?"

* Confirm recording permission (audio only, deleted after synthesis).
* Confirm right to skip any question and to withdraw at any time.
* Share the one-pager describing the catalogue (current scope, not a sales
  pitch).

### 1.2 Warm-up questions

1. What's your current role and which frameworks do you work with most
   (SOC-2, ISO 27001<sup class="ref">[<a href="#ref-4">4</a>]</sup>, PCI-DSS, HIPAA<sup class="ref">[<a href="#ref-8">8</a>]</sup>, GDPR<sup class="ref">[<a href="#ref-2">2</a>]</sup>, SOX<sup class="ref">[<a href="#ref-6">6</a>]</sup>, NIS2<sup class="ref">[<a href="#ref-1">1</a>]</sup>, DORA<sup class="ref">[<a href="#ref-3">3</a>]</sup>, other)?
2. Roughly how many audits/assessments/inspections have you led in the last
   12 months?
3. Think of the most recent one — what did the evidence-pack the client gave
   you actually look like (format, volume, source systems)?

## 2. Evidence-pack reality (10 min)

Goal: ground-truth what auditors actually accept vs. reject.

1. When a client says "here is the evidence for control X," what physical
   artefacts do you look for? (screenshots, exports, log extracts, saved
   searches, dashboards, runbooks, change tickets, meeting minutes, other.)
2. What is the single most common reason you reject or push back on an
   evidence pack?
3. What proportion of evidence packs you see are clearly generated by a SIEM
   or log-management platform versus manually assembled? Has that shifted in
   the last 2-3 years?
4. What evidence formats are **disqualifying**? (e.g. modifiable Word docs,
   undated screenshots, logs without timestamps, un-signed attestations.)
5. Do you have a hard preference for timestamp format, time zone, log source
   attribution, or signing? Please be specific.

## 3. Framework-specific probes (10 min)

Pick the 1-2 frameworks the interviewee works with most and ask:

### 3.1 SOC-2 (Trust Services Criteria 2017)

1. Which TSC criterion is the hardest to demonstrate with technical
   evidence? (E.g. CC7.2 monitoring, CC7.3 incident response, CC6.x logical
   access.)
2. If you saw a "Splunk evidence pack" claiming CC7.2 coverage, what would
   you expect in it at minimum?
3. How do you handle a control that the client "detects violation of" rather
   than "satisfies" — is that language useful to you?

### 3.2 PCI-DSS v4.x (QSA variant)

1. Which 10.2.x sub-requirement is most often under-evidenced?
2. Do you accept daily log-review evidence that is produced by a scheduled
   SIEM search rather than a human sign-off? Under what conditions?
3. What does "file integrity monitoring" evidence actually look like in a
   passing ROC? (e.g. hash database + change diff, or something else.)

### 3.3 GDPR / UK-GDPR (DPO variant)

1. How does a supervisory authority actually test Article 32 technical
   measures — are they asking for proof of detection, proof of response,
   both, or something else?
2. When you review a DPIA's "security of processing" section, do you trust
   SIEM dashboards as evidence or do you demand raw logs?
3. Breach-notification: how do you validate the "72-hour" clock — what
   timestamp do you consider authoritative?

### 3.4 HIPAA Security Rule (for US covered entities / business associates)

1. For §164.312(b) audit controls, what level of log retention and review
   cadence do you expect to see?
2. Do you distinguish between "technical safeguards" evidence and "admin
   safeguards" evidence — and where do Splunk dashboards sit in your mind?
3. How do you handle cloud-hosted PHI — does the audit-log expectation
   change between on-prem and AWS/Azure/GCP?

### 3.5 ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (certification auditor variant)

1. When assessing Annex A.8.15 (Logging) and A.8.16 (Monitoring activities),
   what triggers a non-conformity vs an observation?
2. Is ISMS evidence ever accepted in a "living dashboard" form, or must it
   be a snapshot at time-of-audit?
3. Do you use any NIST / ISO Informative References crosswalks when
   evaluating Annex A coverage?

### 3.6 SOX / ITGC (external auditor / PCAOB variant)

1. For ITGC logging controls, what's the bar for demonstrating that audit
   logs are complete and have not been tampered with?
2. Does your firm accept SIEM-generated evidence directly, or must it be
   transcribed into the auditor's workpaper system?
3. Which ITGCs do you most often find under-tested? (Change mgmt, access,
   backup/restore, operations.)

### 3.7 NIS2 / DORA (EU critical-infrastructure / financial-services variant)

1. How prescriptive are national supervisors about the form of monitoring
   evidence?
2. Does DORA's operational-resilience "ICT incident classification" require
   evidence different from a generic SIEM alert history?
3. How does supply-chain / ICT third-party evidence differ from
   first-party?

## 4. Catalogue value test (10 min)

Show a sample use case from the catalogue on screen
(`UC-22.35.1` — audit-log continuity).

1. Reading just the title and description, could you tell which of *your*
   frameworks it evidences?
2. The catalogue lists clause-level mappings with an "assurance" label
   (`full | partial | contributing`). Is that useful? What language would
   you prefer?
3. The catalogue also distinguishes `satisfies` vs `detects-violation-of`.
   Does that distinction match how you think?
4. If the catalogue produced, per UC, a small evidence pack (saved-search
   definition, dashboard screenshot, fixture data, OSCAL component
   definition, audit trail of when it last ran) — which of those would you
   actually use, and in what order?
5. What's missing from the UC schema for it to be useful in your workflow?

## 5. Exit & next steps (5 min)

1. Would you be willing to review a short follow-up artefact
   (an evidence-pack PDF) in the next 4 weeks? (Yes/No/Maybe.)
2. Anything you wish I had asked?
3. Can we quote (anonymously) your phrasing for specific points? Any
   sentences you want struck from the record?

## 6. Note-taking rubric

Capture answers under these stable fields so they can be aggregated:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `persona` | QSA / SOC-2 / ISO lead auditor / DPO / SOX ITGC / NIS2-DORA / Other |
| `frameworks_primary` | list of primary frameworks interviewee works with |
| `evidence_format_musts` | mandatory fields, formats, timestamps |
| `evidence_format_dealbreakers` | things that cause a reject |
| `schema_reactions.assurance_vocab` | feedback on full/partial/contributing |
| `schema_reactions.mode_vocab` | feedback on satisfies/detects-violation-of |
| `schema_reactions.missing_fields` | fields they want added to UC schema |
| `catalogue_value_rating` | 1–5, with rationale |
| `followup_willing` | yes/no/maybe |
| `quote_consent` | yes/no |
| `anonymised_quotes` | list of strings |

Synthesis (single pass after all interviews) populates
`docs/auditor-research/findings.md` using this same rubric.

## 7. Reference material to have on hand

* One-pager description of the catalogue (without branding / sales).
* Sample evidence pack (PDF) generated from `UC-22.35.1`.
* `schemas/uc.schema.json` (for the "missing fields" question, section 4.5).
* `data/regulations.draft.json` so the interviewer can speak with clause-
  level specificity when asked.
