# Governance

This is a one-maintainer project. The catalogue is volunteer-driven; nothing here is a corporate commitment.

## Who decides what

Decisions are made by the lead maintainer (`@fenre`, see [`.github/CODEOWNERS`](.github/CODEOWNERS)). For non-trivial changes — schema breaks, category removals, build-pipeline rewrites that forks cannot easily follow — open a GitHub issue first so the rationale and the trade-offs are visible. There's no formal RFC clock; reasonable feedback time, then a decision.

If a co-maintainer ever joins, this section will be rewritten and CODEOWNERS will gain path-specific rules. Until then the workflow is intentionally lightweight.

## Cadence and capacity

Release cadence depends on standing capacity. The calibration — what gets shipped in full / reduced / solo mode — lives in [`docs/capacity-and-staffing.md`](docs/capacity-and-staffing.md). External consumers planning against the roadmap should read that doc before assuming a schedule.

The per-phase rollback contract lives in [`docs/rollback-playbook.md`](docs/rollback-playbook.md).

## Conflict of interest

The lead maintainer is not employed by Splunk, by any vendor whose TA is referenced, or by any regulator whose framework is represented. If that changes, the disclosure goes in [`.github/CODEOWNERS`](.github/CODEOWNERS) as a comment.

## Non-goals

- This project does **not** aim to become a foundation-hosted / CNCF-style effort. The catalogue stays intentionally small-team and opinionated.
- Commercial sponsorships and bounties are not accepted. PRs that promote vendors beyond what a use case legitimately requires are closed.

## Amendments

Edits to this document go through the same lightweight process as any other change: open a PR, the lead maintainer reviews. Substantial rewrites that change roles or decision-making should be flagged in the PR title.

## Contact

For governance questions that cannot be discussed in a public issue, contact the lead maintainer at **fsudmann@gmail.com**.
