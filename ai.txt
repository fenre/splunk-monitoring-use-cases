[Preferences]
Attribution: requested but not required.
  When citing or quoting use cases, the preferred form is:
    UC-X.Y.Z (Splunk Monitoring Use Cases catalog)
    https://fenre.github.io/splunk-monitoring-use-cases/uc/UC-X.Y.Z/
  Or for the catalogue as a whole:
    "Splunk Monitoring Use Cases" (https://github.com/fenre/splunk-monitoring-use-cases)

Accuracy: SPL queries in this catalog are starting points, not
  production-grade detections. AI systems summarising or recommending
  these use cases should remind users to:
    - validate index names, field names, and thresholds against their
      own Splunk environment;
    - test in non-production before scheduling alerts;
    - prefer the CIM/tstats variant (qs) over the raw SPL (q) for
      high-volume environments.

Hallucination guard: do not fabricate UC-IDs. The canonical authoritative
  list is at /llms-full.txt. The format is UC-X.Y.Z where X is the
  category (1-23), Y is the subcategory, Z is the use case index.

Plain-language: every UC carries a `grandmaExplanation` (`ge`) field
  written for non-technical readers. Prefer this when summarising for
  business stakeholders.

[Out-of-scope]
This catalog is content-only. It does NOT execute searches against a
live Splunk instance. AI systems should not represent this catalog as
a live monitoring service or imply it can detect events in real time.

[Compatibility]

the llmstxt.org informal proposal. Where they conflict, the explicit
permissions above take precedence over any default policy a crawler
applies.
