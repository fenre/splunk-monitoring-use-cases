# UC-10.16.1 · Attack Surface Change Trending

## Description

Tracking newly exposed services and listening ports over time reveals shadow IT, misconfigurations, and scan gaps before attackers find them. Sudden increases in open ports or services often precede breaches or follow failed change control.

## Value

Tracking newly exposed services and listening ports over time reveals shadow IT, misconfigurations, and scan gaps before attackers find them. Sudden increases in open ports or services often precede breaches or follow failed change control.

## Implementation

Normalize host and port fields per your scanner TA; backfill a KV store of first-seen `(host, port, protocol)` tuples and flag deltas as “new exposure.” Run weekly after patch cycles. Exclude approved scanner ranges from “new” logic. Join high-risk ports to a lookup for prioritization. Schedule the search after nightly import jobs complete.

## Detailed Implementation

Prerequisites

• Ensure the following data sources are available: `index=main` vuln scanner assets (`sourcetype=qualys*`, `nessus*`, `vuln:*`), optional `sourcetype=stash` nightly exposure summaries; `Intrusion_Detection` for correlated context.
• For app installation, inputs.conf, and Splunk directory layout, see the Implementation guide: docs/implementation-guide.md

Step 1 — Configure data collection
Normalize host and port fields per your scanner TA; backfill a KV store of first-seen `(host, port, protocol)` tuples and flag deltas as “new exposure.” Run weekly after patch cycles. Exclude approved scanner ranges from “new” logic. Join high-risk ports to a lookup for prioritization. Schedule the search after nightly import jobs complete.

Step 2 — Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
index=main (sourcetype="qualys:asset" OR sourcetype="tenable:nessus" OR sourcetype="vuln:asset")
| eval port_key=coalesce(dest_port, port, listening_port)
| eval svc_key=coalesce(protocol, "tcp") . ":" . port_key
| bin _time span=1d
| stats dc(svc_key) as distinct_services dc(host) as hosts_affected by _time
| timechart span=1w max(distinct_services) as peak_exposed_services max(hosts_affected) as hosts_with_exposure
```
