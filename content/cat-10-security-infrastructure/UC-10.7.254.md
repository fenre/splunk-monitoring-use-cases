## Detailed Implementation

Enterprise Security Detection Rule

"Cisco Isovalent - Kprobe Spike" is a Hunting detection designed for analyst-driven threat hunting rather than automated alerting, sourced from the Splunk Enterprise Security Content Update (ESCU). Hunting detections are not intended for automated alerting. They are run on-demand or on a low-frequency schedule by security analysts investigating specific threat hypotheses.

Prerequisites

• Splunk Enterprise Security 7.x or later with the ES Content Update (ESCU) app installed and up to date.
• Data sources: Cisco Isovalent Process Kprobe. Must be ingested into Splunk and normalized to the Common Information Model (CIM) via the appropriate Technology Add-on.
• MITRE ATT&CK: T1068. Review the ATT&CK matrix for adjacent techniques to identify detection coverage gaps in your environment.

Deployment

1. In Enterprise Security, navigate to Configure → Content → Content Management.
2. Search for "Cisco Isovalent - Kprobe Spike" or filter by Analytic Story.
3. Hunting detections are typically left disabled for automated scheduling. Instead, run them on-demand from the Search bar or schedule at low frequency (daily or weekly).
4. Review results manually — hunting detections cast a wider net and expect analyst judgment to separate signal from noise.
5. When results warrant further investigation, create a Notable Event manually or initiate your incident response workflow.

## SPL

```spl
`cisco_isovalent` process_kprobe.action!=""
    | bin _time span=5m | rename process_kprobe.parent.pod.name as pod_name
    | stats count as kprobe_count
            values(process_kprobe.function_name) as functions
            values(process_kprobe.process.binary) as binaries
            values(process_kprobe.args{}.string_arg) as args
      by pod_name _time
    | where kprobe_count > 10 | `cisco_isovalent___kprobe_spike_filter`
```
