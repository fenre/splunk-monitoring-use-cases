# UC-21.10.1 · Claims Processing Cycle Time Monitoring

## Description

End-to-end cycle time from FNOL to settlement drives customer satisfaction and loss adjustment expense; segmenting by line of business exposes bottlenecks in adjuster queues and vendor turnaround.

## Value

End-to-end cycle time from FNOL to settlement drives customer satisfaction and loss adjustment expense; segmenting by line of business exposes bottlenecks in adjuster queues and vendor turnaround.

## Implementation

Normalize epoch fields from the claims platform; exclude reopened claims with a flag if present. Tune SLAs by LOB. Pair with staffing dashboards for operational planning—not banking fraud (Cat 10.12). **Domain context:** Cycle time KPIs vary by line (auto physical damage vs workers comp vs liability)—publish LOB-specific targets; regulators may review unfair claims practices in some jurisdictions. **Splunk:** `lower(status)="settled"` must match platform values (`Closed`, `Paid`); use a lookup for canonical statuses.

Step 2 — Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
index=insurance sourcetype="claims:lifecycle" lower(status)="settled"
| eval cycle_d=(settled_epoch-opened_epoch)/86400
| where isnotnull(cycle_d) AND cycle_d >= 0
| bin _time span=1w
| stats avg(cycle_d) as avg_days, perc90(cycle_d) as p90_days, count as claims by lob, _time
| where avg_days > 30 OR p90_days > 60
| eval avg_days_r=round(avg_days,1), p90_days_r=round(p90_days,1)
| table _time, lob, claims, avg_days_r, p90_days_r
```
