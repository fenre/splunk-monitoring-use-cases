# UC-23.7.4 · Backlog Age and Breach Risk Forecast

## Description

Highlights tickets that have been open longer than your policy allows before they breach service promises. We help capacity planners see how much work is aging so they can add shifts or shift topics before customers feel ignored.

## Value

Highlights tickets that have been open longer than your policy allows before they breach service promises. We help capacity planners see how much work is aging so they can add shifts or shift topics before customers feel ignored.

## Implementation

(1) Ingest open incidents with accurate opened timestamps from ServiceNow; (2) align `sla_target_hours` with your contractual response and resolve clocks; (3) schedule every two hours and route “AT RISK” queues to team leads before breaches hit customer reports.

## Detailed Implementation

Prerequisites
• Install and configure the required add-on or app: Splunk Add-on for ServiceNow (Splunkbase 1928).
• Ensure the following data sources are available: `index=itsm` `sourcetype="snow:incident"` (number, opened_at, closed_at, priority, assignment_group).
• For app installation, inputs.conf, and Splunk directory layout, see the Implementation guide: docs/implementation-guide.md

Step 1 — Configure data collection
(1) Ingest open incidents with accurate opened timestamps from ServiceNow; (2) align `sla_target_hours` with your contractual response and resolve clocks; (3) schedule every two hours and route “AT RISK” queues to team leads before breaches hit customer reports.

Step 2 — Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
index=itsm sourcetype="snow:incident" state!="Closed" earliest=-30d
| eval opened_epoch=strptime(opened_at, "%Y-%m-%d %H:%M:%S")
| eval age_hours=round((now()-opened_epoch)/3600,1)
| eval sla_target_hours=case(priority="1",4, priority="2",8, priority="3",24, 1=1,72)
| eval pct_of_sla=round(100*age_hours/sla_target_hours,0)
| eval breach_risk=case(pct_of_sla>=100,"BREACHED", pct_of_sla>=80,"AT RISK", 1=1,"OK")
| stats count as tickets, avg(age_hours) as avg_age_h, max(age_hours) as max_age_h by assignment_group, breach_risk
| sort assignment_group, breach_risk
| table assignment_group, breach_risk, tickets, avg_age_h, max_age_h
```
