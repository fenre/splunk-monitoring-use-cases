# UC-22.8.29 ¬∑ SOC 2 C1 ‚Äî Confidential Information Disposal and Secure Destruction Evidence

## Description

C1 addresses confidential information. Tracking destruction certificates, crypto-shredding events, and DLP ‚Äúsecure delete‚Äù workflows demonstrates media and logical disposal met policy when assets retired or contracts ended.

## Value

Gives the responsible team auditor-ready evidence for SOC 2 compliance by automating regulatory compliance evidence using indexes cmdb, compliance, endpoint and sourcetypes cmdb:asset, disposal:cert. Replaces manual evidence gathering with a continuous Splunk search that produces structured, time-stamped artefacts suitable for regulatory examination and internal audit review.

## Implementation

(1) Flip logic per policy: flag retirements without cert within 30 days; (2) restrict `classification=confidential` scope; (3) integrate ITAD vendor feed; (4) legal hold exceptions via `legal_hold_assets.csv`; (5) annual sample for auditors with cert PDF links (out of band). Indexes required: `index=cmdb`, `index=compliance`, `index=endpoint`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=cmdb:asset`, `sourcetype=disposal:cert`.

Step 1 ‚Äî Configure data collection
(1) Flip logic per policy: flag retirements without cert within 30 days; (2) restrict `classification=confidential` scope; (3) integrate ITAD vendor feed; (4) legal hold exceptions via `legal_hold_assets.csv`; (5) annual sample for auditors with cert PDF links (out of band).

Step 2 ‚Äî Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
index=compliance sourcetype="disposal:cert" earliest=-365d
| eval destroyed=strptime(destroyed_ts,"%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%SZ")
| join type=left asset_id [
    search index=cmdb sourcetype="cmdb:asset" earliest=-400d
    | fields asset_id, retired_date, classification
    | eval retired=strptime(retired_date,"%Y-%m-%d")
  ]
| where retired>relative_time(destroyed,"+30d@d")
| table asset_id, classification, retired, destroyed, cert_number
```
