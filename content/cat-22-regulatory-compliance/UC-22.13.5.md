# 22.13.5 — Security Policy Exception Register with Expiration Tracking

**Criticality:** high | **Difficulty:** intermediate | **Status:** N/A

## Description

We prevent policy exceptions from silently exceeding approved dates so security management controls stay within delegated risk acceptance.

## Value

Gives the responsible team auditor-ready evidence for NERC CIP compliance by automating regulatory compliance evidence using indexes grc and sourcetypes cip:policy_exception, cip:policy_exception`. Replaces manual evidence gathering with a continuous Splunk search that produces structured, time-stamped artefacts suitable for regulatory examination and internal audit review.

## SPL

```spl
index=grc sourcetype=cip:policy_exception status="active" earliest=-120d
| eval exp_epoch=strptime(expires_on,"%Y-%m-%d")
| eval expired=if(now()>exp_epoch,1,0)
| eval days_left=round((exp_epoch-now())/86400,1)
| where expired=1 OR days_left<14
| table exception_id, control_ref, owner, expires_on, expired, days_left
```

## Implementation

(1) Ingest the exception register via HEC or DB Connect on change. (2) Map `control_ref` to CIP-003 policies. (3) Alert at fourteen days and on expiration. (4) Require closure events to flip status. Indexes required: `index=grc`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=cip:policy_exception`, `sourcetype=cip:policy_exception``.
