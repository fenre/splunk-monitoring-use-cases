# 22.35.2 — Log tamper detection via write-once-read-many chain-of-custody

**Criticality:** critical | **Difficulty:** advanced | **Status:** verified

## Description

Compares the cryptographic bucket hash across indexer peer nodes and raises a finding whenever the primary and its replicas disagree. A divergence is the earliest evidence of on-disk tampering or a replication-layer fault — both of which break the audit chain-of-custody that GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS, SOC 2, and SOX ITGC assume.

## Value

Produces defensible chain-of-custody evidence: auditors can be shown a 30-day view in which every bucket was either green or had a timestamped remediation ticket.

## SPL

```spl
| rest /services/cluster/master/buckets splunk_server=* | eval primary_hash=coalesce('primaries{}.bucket_flags.hash','bucket_hash') | stats values(primary_hash) as hashes dc(primary_hash) as uniques by index, bucket_id | where uniques>1
| eval divergence_detected_at=strftime(now(),"%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%SZ")
| table divergence_detected_at index bucket_id hashes
```

## Implementation

(1) Enable indexer-cluster replication with searchFactor≥2, replicationFactor≥3; (2) schedule the REST probe every 5 minutes; (3) pipe hits to compliance_summary and to ITSI as a KPI; (4) escalate to CISO on any bucket divergence older than 30 minutes.

## Visualization

Heat-map of indexes × buckets over 30d (green/red); single value for '% of buckets verified'; time-chart of open divergences.

- **HIPAA Security** 2013-final: §164.312(c)(1) (full) — §164.312(c)(1) Integrity requires mechanisms to corroborate that ePHI has not been altered. Hash-chain verification over the audit store is a direct implementation.
- **PCI DSS** v4.0: 10.5 (full) — PCI 10.5 requires audit-log integrity controls; WORM + hash-chain is explicitly called out as an acceptable method in the v4.0 Guidance.
- **SOC 2** 2017 TSC: CC7.2 (partial) — CC7.2 covers anomaly detection; log-integrity verification contributes to the evaluated-events program but additional processing controls apply.
- **SOX ITGC** PCAOB AS 2201: ITGC.Logging.Continuity (contributing) — A broken hash chain in an audit period is a material ITGC exception; this UC surfaces the signal and writes it to the audit-evidence index.

- **LGPD** Lei nº 13.709/2018: Art.46 (partial) — Uplifted from contributing to partial: UC provides meaningful monitoring evidence for Art.46.

## Known False Positives

Planned rolling restarts of indexer peers can briefly show hash mismatches during re-balancing. Use maintenance_windows.csv and a 10-minute grace window.
