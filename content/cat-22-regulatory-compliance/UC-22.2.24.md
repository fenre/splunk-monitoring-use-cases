# 22.2.24 — NIS2 Business Continuity and ICT Continuity Evidence

**Criticality:** critical | **Difficulty:** intermediate | **Status:** N/A

## Description

Business continuity, crisis management, and ICT continuity are explicit Article 21 themes. This use case monitors declared RTO/RPO adherence during real incidents and exercises, combining ITSI service degradation windows with BCP test completion records.

## Value

Gives the responsible team auditor-ready evidence for NIS2 compliance by automating regulatory compliance evidence using indexes itsi_summary, itsm. Replaces manual evidence gathering with a continuous Splunk search that produces structured, time-stamped artefacts suitable for regulatory examination and internal audit review.

## SPL

```spl
| inputlookup nis2_bcp_test_register.csv
| eval test_date_epoch=strptime(last_tabletop_date,"%Y-%m-%d")
| eval months_since=round((now()-test_date_epoch)/2592000,1)
| where months_since > 12
| join service_name [
    search index=itsi_summary earliest=-90d
    | where severity IN ("Critical","High")
    | stats sum(alert_duration) as downtime_seconds by service_name
]
| eval rto_seconds=rto_minutes*60
| eval rto_breach=if(downtime_seconds > rto_seconds, 1, 0)
| table service_name, months_since, downtime_seconds, rto_seconds, rto_breach
```

## Implementation

(1) Record BCP/ICT continuity test dates per critical service; (2) map services to ITSI entities; (3) alert when annual tabletop is overdue; (4) measure downtime during sev1 incidents against RTO; (5) integrate with UC-22.2.17 for backup evidence. Indexes required: `index=itsi_summary`, `index=itsm`; Lookups: `nis2_bcp_test_register`, `nis2_bcp_test_register.csv`.

## Visualization

Table (overdue tests and RTO breaches), Timeline (tests and incidents), Single value (services breaching RTO). Key fields: `downtime_seconds`, `months_since`, `rto_breach`, `rto_seconds`, `service_name`, `test_date_epoch`.

## Compliance

- **NIS2** Directive (EU) 2022/2555: Art.21(2)(c) (partial) — Uplifted from contributing: SPL directly addresses Art.21(2)(c) requirements with specific, actionable monitoring.

## Known False Positives

Administrative tasks, scheduled jobs or platform updates can match this pattern — correlate with change management, maintenance windows and user role before raising severity.
