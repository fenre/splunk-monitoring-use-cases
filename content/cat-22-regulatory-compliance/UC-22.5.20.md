# UC-22.5.20 · MiFID II Market Abuse — Cross-Venue Wash Trade Risk Linking by Beneficial Owner

## Description

Wash trades may split across accounts and venues. Linking executions by `beneficial_owner_id` and near-equal opposing volumes within short windows supports cross-venue surveillance beyond single-broker silos.

## Value

Gives the responsible team auditor-ready evidence for MiFID II compliance by automating regulatory compliance evidence using indexes trading and sourcetypes ems:fill. Replaces manual evidence gathering with a continuous Splunk search that produces structured, time-stamped artefacts suitable for regulatory examination and internal audit review.

## Implementation

(1) Tune time bin and imbalance; (2) enrich `bo_id` from KYC; (3) exclude bona fide internalizers per policy; (4) STOR escalation path; (5) coordinate with surveillance vendor rules. Indexes required: `index=trading`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=ems:fill`.

Step 2 — Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
index=trading sourcetype="ems:fill" earliest=-1d
| eval t=strptime(fill_ts,"%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%SZ")
| bin t span=5s
| stats sum(eval(if(side="B",qty,0))) as buy_qty, sum(eval(if(side="S",qty,0))) as sell_qty by bo_id, symbol, t, venue
| eval imbalance=abs(buy_qty-sell_qty)
| where buy_qty>0 AND sell_qty>0 AND imbalance< (buy_qty+sell_qty)*0.05
| sort - buy_qty
```
