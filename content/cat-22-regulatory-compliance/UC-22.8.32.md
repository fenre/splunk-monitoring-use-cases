# 22.8.32 — SOC 2 CC6.7 — System boundary & data-transmission control: unapproved egress destinations

**Criticality:** high | **Difficulty:** intermediate | **Status:** verified

## Description

Evaluates outbound flows against the per-service approved-egress registry. A flow to an unapproved ASN or country is a direct CC6.7 boundary violation.

## Value

Provides the evidence SOC 2 auditors want: proof that outbound flows are governed and deviations are captured. Supports reduction of shadow SaaS risk.

## SPL

```spl
index=netfw sourcetype=pan:traffic action=allow direction=outbound earliest=-15m
| rename src AS src_ip dest AS dest_ip
| lookup service_by_src.csv src_ip OUTPUT src_service
| lookup asn_country.csv ip=dest_ip OUTPUT asn AS dest_asn country AS dest_country
| lookup approved_egress.csv src_service dest_asn OUTPUT approved
| where isnull(approved) OR approved!="yes"
| stats count sum(bytes_out) AS bytes BY src_service dest_ip dest_asn dest_country
```

## Implementation

(1) Enrich Palo Alto traffic with service/ASN/country; (2) maintain approved_egress.csv; (3) schedule UC every 15m; (4) hit opens a ticket to service owner; (5) 48h SLA; (6) weekly review by Platform team. Indexes required: `index=netfw`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=pan:traffic`, `sourcetype=pan:traffic;`.

## Visualization

Geo map of unapproved destinations, bar chart by service, single value 'unapproved egress flows (1h)'. Key fields: `bytes`, `dest_asn`, `dest_country`, `dest_ip`, `src_ip`, `src_service`.

## Compliance

- **SOC-2** 2017 TSC: CC6.7 (full) — CC6.7 Information transmission and disposal — unapproved egress is the direct failure.
- **ISO/IEC 27001** 2022: A.8.23 (partial) — ISO/IEC 27001:2022 A.8.23 Web filtering — contributing.
- **PCI-DSS** v4.0: 1.3.1 (partial) — PCI-DSS 1.3.1 traffic to and from the CDE — contributing.
- **DORA** Regulation (EU) 2022/2554: Art.7 (full) — Uplifted to full: SPL provides comprehensive, standalone evidence for Art.7 with specific data sources and actionable thresholds.

## Known False Positives

Public CDNs and package mirrors that use anycast may produce transient country churn — allowlist by ASN in approved_egress.csv.
