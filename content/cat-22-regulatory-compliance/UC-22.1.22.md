# UC-22.1.22 · GDPR Access Control Review and Privileged Access Evidence

## Description

Article 32(1)(b) requires measures to ensure ongoing confidentiality, including controlling access to personal data. This use case reviews who can access GDPR-scoped systems — flagging excessive admin grants, dormant privileged accounts, and role changes that expand access beyond ROPA-documented purposes — giving auditors repeatable evidence of access governance.

## Value

Article 32(1)(b) requires measures to ensure ongoing confidentiality, including controlling access to personal data. This use case reviews who can access GDPR-scoped systems — flagging excessive admin grants, dormant privileged accounts, and role changes that expand access beyond ROPA-documented purposes — giving auditors repeatable evidence of access governance.

## Implementation

(1) Map hosts and SaaS apps in `gdpr_system_access_matrix.csv` with approved IAM roles; (2) ingest IdP group and role assignment events; (3) correlate successful authentications to personal-data systems; (4) alert when users access GDPR systems without an approved role or after a recent privilege grant; (5) export monthly access review packets for data owners.

Step 1 — Configure data collection
(1) Map hosts and SaaS apps in `gdpr_system_access_matrix.csv` with approved IAM roles; (2) ingest IdP group and role assignment events; (3) correlate successful authentications to personal-data systems; (4) alert when users access GDPR systems without an approved role or after a recent privilege grant; (5) export monthly access review packets for data owners.

Step 2 — Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
| tstats `summariesonly` latest(Authentication.user) as user latest(Authentication.app) as app
  from datamodel=Authentication.Authentication
  where Authentication.action="success" AND earliest=-30d
  by Authentication.dest
| rename Authentication.* as *
| lookup gdpr_system_access_matrix.csv system_hostname AS dest OUTPUT data_classification, approved_roles
| where data_classification IN ("personal_data","special_category")
| join type=left user [
    search index=okta sourcetype="OktaIM2:log" eventType="group.privilege.grant" earliest=-30d
    | stats latest(_time) as last_grant by target_user_id
    | rename target_user_id as user
]
| where isnotnull(last_grant)
| table dest, user, app, data_classification, approved_roles, last_grant
```
