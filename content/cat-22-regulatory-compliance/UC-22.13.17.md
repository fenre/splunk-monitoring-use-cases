# 22.13.17 — Denied ESP Traversal Bursts Followed by Allowed Sessions

**Criticality:** critical | **Difficulty:** advanced | **Status:** N/A

## Description

We correlate dense deny activity at the ESP with subsequent allows to prioritize perimeter probing that may indicate misconfiguration or attack.

## Value

Gives the responsible team auditor-ready evidence for NERC CIP compliance by automating regulatory compliance evidence using indexes network and sourcetypes pan:traffic, pan:traffic`. Replaces manual evidence gathering with a continuous Splunk search that produces structured, time-stamped artefacts suitable for regulatory examination and internal audit review.

## SPL

```spl
index=network sourcetype=pan:traffic dest_zone="ESP_PERIMETER" earliest=-4h
| eval outcome=if(action="deny","deny","allow")
| bin _time span=5m
| stats sum(eval(if(outcome=="deny",1,0))) as denies sum(eval(if(outcome=="allow",1,0))) as allows by src, _time
| where denies>40 AND allows>0
| sort - denies
```

## CIM SPL

```spl
| tstats summariesonly=t sum(All_Traffic.bytes_in) as agg_value from datamodel=Network_Traffic.All_Traffic by All_Traffic.src span=5m | sort - agg_value
```

## Implementation

(1) Calibrate deny thresholds per ESP. (2) Enrich with threat log if separate sourcetype. (3) Feed ES correlation. (4) Document benign scanner ranges in lookup. Indexes required: `index=network`.
