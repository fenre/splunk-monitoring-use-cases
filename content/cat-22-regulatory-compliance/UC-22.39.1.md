# 22.39.1 — Multi-regulator breach-notification SLA tracker (24h NIS2 / 72h GDPR / 72h HIPAA)

**Criticality:** critical | **Difficulty:** advanced | **Status:** verified

## Description

Tracks every incident that has crossed the 'breach' threshold and reports on the notification clock per applicable regulator. Imminent breaches trigger a warning; missed deadlines trigger a critical notable and a board-level communication.

## Value

Most enforcement actions against supervisory breaches (ICO, DPC, BaFin) focus on notification timeliness. This UC turns the obligation into a visible KPI instead of a post-facto audit finding.

## SPL

```spl
| inputlookup incident_register.csv
| where classification="breach"
| eval applicable_regs=split(applicable_regs,",")
| mvexpand applicable_regs
| lookup sla_catalog.csv regulator as applicable_regs OUTPUT sla_hours
| eval deadline=strptime(classified_at,"%Y-%m-%dT%H:%M:%SZ")+sla_hours*3600
| join incident_id applicable_regs [search index=notifications sourcetype=regulator:submission | fields incident_id applicable_regs tracking_id submitted_at]
| eval status=case(isnull(tracking_id) AND now()>deadline, "breach", isnull(tracking_id) AND (deadline-now())<5400, "imminent", isnotnull(tracking_id), "submitted")
| table incident_id applicable_regs deadline submitted_at status
```

## Implementation

(1) Ingest classifications from ServiceNow / ES incident index; (2) maintain sla_catalog.csv with regulator → SLA hours; (3) ingest notification-portal tracking IDs; (4) schedule every 15 min; (5) integrate with SOAR to auto-escalate. Indexes required: `index=notifications`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=regulator:submission`.

## Visualization

Gantt of incidents vs SLA deadlines, single-value 'incidents notified on time (90d)', breakdown by regulator.

## Compliance

- **GDPR** 2016/679: Art.33 (full) — Art.33(1) requires notification to the supervisory authority without undue delay and where feasible within 72 hours; the UC measures elapsed time from classification to submission evidence.
- **NIS2** Directive (EU) 2022/2555: Art.23 (full) — NIS2 Art.23 imposes an early-warning obligation within 24h and an incident notification within 72h; the UC evidences both clocks for every eligible incident.
- **DORA** Regulation (EU) 2022/2554: Art.19 (full) — DORA Art.19 requires major ICT-related incident reporting; SLA tracking against intermediate and final reports is the canonical evidence.
- **HIPAA Security** 2013-final: §164.308(a)(6) (partial) — §164.308(a)(6) security incident procedures include notification duties per §164.400-414; the UC surfaces the timer but breach assessment is decided by the privacy officer.
- **APPI** 2022 amendments: Art.26 (partial) — Derived from GDPR@2016/679 Art.33 (parent assurance='full'). Clause mapped per data/regulations.json derivesFrom graph; inherited assurance degraded one step.
- **LGPD** Lei nº 13.709/2018: Art.48 (partial) — Derived from GDPR@2016/679 Art.33 (parent assurance='full'). Clause mapped per data/regulations.json derivesFrom graph; inherited assurance degraded one step. Divergence recorded — SME review should confirm scope fit.
- **Swiss nFADP** 2020 revision: Art.24 (partial) — Derived from GDPR@2016/679 Art.33 (parent assurance='full'). Clause mapped per data/regulations.json derivesFrom graph; inherited assurance degraded one step. Divergence recorded — SME review should confirm scope fit.
- **UK GDPR** post-Brexit: Art.33 (full) — Uplifted to full: SPL provides comprehensive, standalone evidence for Art.33 with specific data sources and actionable thresholds.

## Known False Positives

Incidents whose submission happens offline with post-hoc upload require a timezone-normalised submitted_at; mis-mapped timezones produce false SLA breaches.
