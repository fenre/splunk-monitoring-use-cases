# 22.35.4 — Log signing chain integrity — cryptographic signature drift on evidence archive

**Criticality:** critical | **Difficulty:** advanced | **Status:** draft

## Description

Continuously verifies that every new block in the write-once evidence archive references the immediately-preceding block by cryptographic hash. A mismatch is definitive evidence of either a faulty archive writer or a deliberate rewrite — either way a finding that regulators treat as a break in the chain of custody.

## Value

Replaces the quarterly 'we sampled 10 logs and checked hashes' audit procedure with a continuous control whose output is either 'zero breaks for the period' or a named remediation record. This is the evidence regulators actually ask for.

## SPL

```spl
index=compliance sourcetype=archive:signer earliest=-1h
| eval block_parent_hash=coalesce(parent_hash,"GENESIS")
| stats values(block_hash) as block_hashes latest(expected_parent_hash) as expected_parent by block_id archive_id
| where expected_parent!=block_parent_hash AND block_parent_hash!="GENESIS"
| table archive_id block_id block_parent_hash expected_parent block_hashes
```

## Implementation

(1) Ensure the archive signer emits a per-block event with block_id, block_hash, parent_hash, and expected_parent_hash; (2) schedule the UC hourly; (3) route hits to compliance_summary; (4) SOAR auto-opens a P1 with the relevant archive owner; (5) run an automated rebuild drill monthly to ensure verification code itself is not silently broken. Indexes required: `index=compliance`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=archive:signer`.

## Visualization

Timeline of verified vs broken blocks, scatter of block_id × hash-mismatch severity, single-value '% of archive blocks verified (30d)'.

## Compliance

- **GDPR** 2016/679: Art.32(1)(b) (partial) — Detects breaks in the integrity component of the trail; confidentiality and availability components need other controls.
- **HIPAA** 2013-final: §164.312(c)(1) (full) — Integrity controls for ePHI are directly implemented by an unbroken hash-chain on the evidence archive.
- **PCI-DSS** v4.0: 10.3.4 (full) — PCI 10.3.4 'file integrity monitoring or change-detection mechanisms on audit logs' is satisfied by a cryptographic-signature chain that emits failures.
- **SOX-ITGC** PCAOB AS 2201: ITGC.Logging.Integrity (contributing) — A broken signature chain is a textbook ITGC deficiency but the control also needs management attestation.
- **UK GDPR** post-Brexit: Art.32(1)(b) (partial) — Uplifted from contributing to partial: UC provides meaningful monitoring evidence for Art.32(1)(b).

## Known False Positives

Archive signer upgrades that rotate the hash function produce a one-off legitimate 'parent-missing' at the rotation boundary — record the rotation in archive_rotations.csv and allow-list by timestamp.
