# UC-22.26.15 · Petroleum facility access control

## Description

Correlates physical badge events with logical sessions into engineering workstations for facility access control.

## Value

Gives the responsible team auditor-ready evidence for NO Petroleumsforskriften compliance by automating regulatory compliance evidence using indexes pacs, vpn and sourcetypes badge:swipe, paloalto:globalprotect. Replaces manual evidence gathering with a continuous Splunk search that produces structured, time-stamped artefacts suitable for regulatory examination and internal audit review.

## Implementation

(1) Maintain supporting lookups (classified hosts, sectors, transfers) aligned to Norwegian regulatory language. (2) Schedule searches with Europe/Oslo time semantics for reporting cutoffs. (3) Route ES notables to the correct national security, OT, or privacy queues. (4) Quarterly false-positive tuning with HR, operations, and legal stakeholders. Indexes required: `index=pacs`, `index=vpn`; Sourcetypes: `sourcetype=badge:swipe`, `sourcetype=paloalto:globalprotect`.

Step 2 — Create the search and alert
Run the following SPL in Search (then save as report or alert; adjust time range and threshold as needed):

```spl
index=pacs sourcetype="badge:swipe" site="NOR-OIL-*" earliest=-24h
| eval day=strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"), user=lower(user)
| stats earliest(_time) as first_in, latest(_time) as last_in by user, day, site
| join type=left user, day [| search index=vpn sourcetype="paloalto:globalprotect" log_type="connect" earliest=-2d
    | eval day=strftime(_time,"%Y-%m-%d"), user=lower(user)
    | stats dc(public_ip) as vpn_ips by user, day]
| where isnull(vpn_ips) AND (last_in-first_in)>3600
| table user, day, site, first_in, last_in, vpn_ips
```
